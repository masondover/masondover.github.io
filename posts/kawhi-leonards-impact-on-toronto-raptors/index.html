Kawhi Leonard’s Impact on the Toronto Raptors

Author

Mason Dover

Published

May 5, 2024

After the Cleveland Cavaliers swept the Toronto Raptors in the second round of the 2018 NBA Playoffs, something needed to change. On July 18, 2018, the Raptors traded DeMar DeRozen, Jakob Poeltl and a 2019 first-round pick to the San Antonio Spurs for Kawhi Leonard and Danny Green.

Toronto traded away their best player, DeMar DeRozen, and took a chance on Kawhi. Leonard sustained a right quadriceps injury the season prior, limiting him to 9 total games.

The injury did not hold Kawhi back and the Toronto Raptors had another great year. The Raptors overcame a second round exit and ended up winning their first NBA Championship.

Even though Kawhi only spent one year with the Raptors, how much of an impact did he have on the Toronto Raptors?

NBA Regular Season games determine which teams qualify for the Playoffs. Coming off of an amazing career with the Spurs, Kawhi needed to show the Raptors what he could do.

Toronto’s trade worked out! The Raptors finished second overall in the NBA. Leonard came into Toronto ready to win and succeeded.

In any basketball game, the offense and defense determine a win or loss. Since Kawhi has won a lot, how has he performed in the offense?

The Raptors traded away their leading scorer for Leonard. Toronto needed Kawhi to fill in the role and lead them to a championship.

Code 
KawhiCareer <- read_csv("KawhiData - KawhiPlayerStats.csv")
KawhiTeams <- read_csv("KawhiData - KawhiTeams.csv")

KawhiSpecificTeams <- KawhiTeams |>
  filter(PRESENT == "YES") |>
  select(Season, Tm, PTS) |>
  rename(
    TeamPts = PTS
  )

KawhiBarTeams <-KawhiCareer |>
  inner_join(KawhiSpecificTeams) |>
  mutate(
    TeamSeason = paste(Tm, Season)
  ) |>
  select(TeamSeason, TeamPts, PTS) |>
  rename(
    Pts = PTS
  ) |>
  pivot_longer(
    cols=ends_with("Pts"), 
    names_to="Type", 
    values_to="Pts")

ggplot() + 
  geom_bar(data=KawhiBarTeams, aes(x=reorder(TeamSeason, Pts), weight=Pts, fill=Type)) + 
  scale_fill_manual(values = c("red", "lightgrey")) +
  coord_flip() +
  labs(title="Leonard Finds Rhythm", 
       subtitle="Kawhi becomes a dual threat when his offensive efficiency improved with the Toronto Raptors.", 
       y= "Total Points", 
       x = "",
       caption="Source: Basketball-Reference.com | By Mason Dover") +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 20, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 10), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=11), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot",
    legend.position = "none"
    )

Until recently, Kawhi’s season with the Toronto Raptors was the most offensively efficient team in his career. Toronto scored 9,384 points that year, which is the most points scored in any Raptors season. Kawhi contributed 1,596 points to the franchise record.

Kawhi led the Raptors in scoring that year with an average of 26.6 points. Almost averaging 10 total points over the next player, Pascal Siakam, Leonard was a big asset on offense for the Raptors.

Now that Kawhi’s offensive skills have been determined, how was his defense with the Raptors?

Kawhi came into the Raptors with amazing defensive skills from the Spurs. The two-time Defensive Player of the Year wanted to shut down the court.

While Kawhi did not earn All-Defensive First Team, he was awarded for his defensive skills that year.

Leonard proved to be a dual threat on the court. The 2019 NBA Champion had a great all-around game that helped the Toronto Raptors win the league.

The potential Hall of Famer had a significant impact on the Raptors. Even though Leonard left the organization, no one can deny what he did for the Raptors.
